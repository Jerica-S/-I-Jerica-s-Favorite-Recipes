Tonkotsu Ramen Recipe

Broth Ingredients

  • 1 pound pigs trotters (skin on)
  • 1 lb. pork bones (Best to incorporate a mix for extra flavor)
  • 1/3 bunch green onion, cut into 3 inch segments
  • 1/3 large yellow onion, cut into quarters
  • 2/3 shallots
  • 2 inch knob ginger
  • 1 garlic clove
  • water to cover

Tare Ingredients

  • vegetable oil
  • 3/4 cup dried anchovies
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tbsp. sake
  • 1 sheet kombu kelp (or "some" bonito flakes)

Noodle Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup baking soda
  • 4oz + 2 tbsp. water
  • 240g all-purpose flour

Broth Instructions

  1. Place 1 lb. of pig trotters and 1 lb. bones into a stock pot & fill pot with water to 2 inches above the ingredients
  2. Bring to a rolling boil for 10 minutes while skimming impurities off the top
  3. Strain bones and rinse with cold water, taking care to remove any impurities then return to pot and cover with 3 inches of water
  4. Bring to a rapid boil on high heat, then immediately lower to the lowest possible boiling temperature
  5. Cover and let boil for 9 hours, stirring once an hour to keep the bones from sticking to the bottom of the pot. Occasionally add water according to reduction.
  6. In the last hour, add 1/3 green onions, the 2/3 of the shallot, 1/2 yellow onion, 2 inch knobs of ginger, and 1 cloves of garlic

Tare Instructions

  1. Heat some vegetable oil over medium-high heat and add 3/4 cups of dried anchovies. Let them cook for 3-4 minutes without stirring, as you want to develop fond at the bottom of the pan
  2. Add 1/2 cup of soy sauce and 1/2 cup of mirin to the mix, as well as the kombu kelp
  3. Remove from heat to let ingredients steep for 5 minutes
  4. Add 2 tbsp sake and place back on heat to burn off alcohol. Set aside

Noodle Instructions

In a mixing bowl add 240g of all purpose flour along with your alkaline water mixture. Stir to combine with a large wooden spoon. You want to make a shaggy but cohesive ball of dough. If needed add a little extra water. Once it’s combined, pat into a disc, dust it with bread flour, and cover. Let it rest for 30 minutes. Once it’s rested for 30 minutes, remove from bowl and lightly dust it with flour. Roll your dough out and then fold into thirds, and then into thirds again. Roll out just a little bit and then wrap in plastic wrap and let rest for 30 more minutes. On a stand mixer attach your pasta attachment. Remove dough from plastic wrap and dust with bread flour. Give it one pass on your pasta rollers widest setting. Fold into thirds, rotate 90° and pass it through again on your pasta press at about a 2 or 3. If it seems to long, give it a chop in half. Dust with bread flour again, before passing though your noodle cutter attachment. Add some more bread flour and twist into little nests on a rimmed baking sheet that’s also been dusted with bread flour. Place noodles in boiling water and cook no longer than 3 minutes checking for doneness at 90 seconds.
  1. Start by baking baking soda in a 250°F oven for 1 hour. Add 2 tsp of mixture with 4 ounces plus 2 Tbsp of water making sure it dissolves completely.
